Welcome to Cork

Cork, the second largest city in Ireland, is renowned for its vibrant food scene, historical landmarks, and scenic waterways.

The History of Cork

Cork's history dates back to the 6th century when it was founded as a monastic settlement. Over the centuries, Cork evolved into a bustling trading port and played a pivotal role in Ireland’s fight for independence. Known as "The Rebel County," Cork's heritage reflects its resilience and cultural richness.

The city’s architecture tells its story—from medieval lanes and Georgian buildings to the vibrant modern quays along the River Lee. Today, Cork combines a rich past with contemporary vibrancy, making it a must-visit destination for history enthusiasts and travelers alike.
