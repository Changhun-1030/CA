Scenic Galway: The Beauty of Ireland's West Coast

A balmy, bohemian city with a love of traditional music – Galway is hard to resist. The spirit of Ireland’s west coast beats through the bustling streets, from mellow old pubs boasting turf and cosy corners, to shops selling Claddagh rings, Aran sweaters and bog-oak carvings.

On the flipside, you’ll find boundary-pushing chefs who are passionate about local ingredients, chic design stores, rolling street music and a seriously vibrant film scene (Galway is a UNESCO City of Film).

Culture

Our country’s fourth-largest city has a unique character that attracts visitors from near and far. But did you know that Galway is known as Ireland’s cultural heart?

This incredible city has so much to offer art lovers, music enthusiasts, theatergoers, and anyone who appreciates a lively and welcoming atmosphere. It’s no surprise, then, that Galway is one of the most popular destinations on our tours in Ireland.

One of the main reasons Galway is known as Ireland’s cultural heart is its vibrant arts and festival scene. Galway hosts several major festivals throughout the year, attracting visitors from all over the world.

The Galway International Arts Festival, which takes place every July, is one of the largest multidisciplinary arts festivals in the world. It features theatre, music, visual arts, and dance performances, making it an incredible experience for anyone lucky enough to attend.

And there’s far more besides! Galway also hosts the Galway Film Fleadh, the Galway Theatre Festival, and the Cuirt International Festival of Literature, to name only a few. These events showcase and celebrate various art forms and provide a fantastic opportunity to immerse yourself in Irish culture.

Food

Galway, a vibrant city on the west coast of Ireland, is renowned for its rich culinary scene, blending traditional Irish fare with contemporary influences. The city offers a delightful mix of seafood, hearty stews, and locally-sourced produce, all complemented by a lively atmosphere. Here are some highlights of Galway's food culture:

Seafood: Galway is famous for its fresh seafood, particularly oysters. The city's location on the Atlantic Ocean provides an abundance of fish and shellfish, including Galway Bay oysters, which are celebrated for their delicate flavor. Many restaurants, such as those in the bustling Galway Fish Market, serve a variety of fresh fish, crab, and mussels.

Irish Stew: A classic Irish dish, Galway's version of Irish stew is hearty and comforting. Made with lamb or beef, potatoes, carrots, onions, and herbs, this dish is a staple of traditional Irish cuisine.

Sodabread and Brown Bread: Traditional Irish breads, like soda bread and brown bread, are commonly served with meals in Galway. These dense, rustic breads are often enjoyed with butter or alongside a hot bowl of soup

Smoked Salmon: Irish smoked salmon is a specialty, and Galway is no exception. Locally sourced salmon is smoked to perfection, often served with crusty bread or in a salad.

Galway Bay Crab and Lobster: The waters around Galway are rich in crab and lobster, and these delicacies are often featured on local menus. Whether boiled, grilled, or served with a rich butter sauce, they are a must-try for seafood lovers.

Scenery

Galway is a picturesque city in Ireland, known for its stunning natural landscapes and vibrant coastal charm. Highlights include Galway Bay, renowned for its breathtaking sunsets, and the Connemara National Park, offering rugged mountains, serene boglands, and scenic hiking trails. The city’s combination of natural beauty and rich cultural heritage makes it a must-visit destination.

Galway Bay

Famous for its stunning sunsets and serene waters, perfect for a coastal stroll or a relaxing evening by the sea.

Connemara National Park

Vast natural landscapes, including mountains, bogs, heaths, and forests, offer hiking trails and stunning panoramic views.
